ADHD Resources

Here are some resources and hacks to help manage ADHD symptoms.

If you can try working for just 10 minutes, you might find it easier to continue working afterwards. Often, starting is the hardest part, and once you've begun, you may find it easier to keep going. Remember to set a timer for 10 minutes and focus solely on the task at hand during that time. After the timer goes off, you can decide whether to continue working or take a break.

Creatine is a popular supplement known for its benefits in enhancing physical performance, particularly in high-intensity exercise. However, emerging research suggests that creatine may also have cognitive benefits, including improved memory and mental fatigue resistance. Some studies indicate that creatine supplementation could help improve cognitive function in individuals with ADHD by enhancing brain energy metabolism. While more research is needed to confirm these effects, taking creatine as part of a balanced diet may offer potential benefits for managing ADHD symptoms. Always consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new supplement.

Regular physical exercise can significantly help individuals with ADHD manage ADHD symptoms. Exercise increases the production of neurotransmitters like dopamine and norepinephrine, which play key roles in attention and focus. Engaging in activities such as aerobic exercise, strength training, or even yoga can improve mood, reduce anxiety, and enhance cognitive function. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate exercise most days of the week. Incorporating physical activity into your daily routine can lead to better symptom management and overall well-being.

Alcohol can significantly worsen ADHD symptoms by disrupting sleep quality, reducing executive function, and interfering with medication effectiveness. It can impair focus, memory, and impulse control—areas already challenging for those with ADHD. Additionally, alcohol affects dopamine levels in the brain, which can exacerbate attention and motivation issues. If you choose to drink, do so in moderation and be mindful of how it affects your symptoms and overall functioning.

Eating close to bedtime can disrupt sleep quality by keeping your digestive system active when your body should be winding down. Poor sleep significantly impacts ADHD symptoms, making focus and emotional regulation even more difficult. Try to finish your last meal at least 4 hours before bed to give your body time to digest. If you need a snack, opt for something light and avoid sugary or caffeinated foods. Better sleep hygiene leads to better symptom management during the day.

The blue light emitted by phones, tablets, and computers suppresses melatonin production, making it harder to fall asleep. For people with ADHD, who often already struggle with sleep issues, this can be particularly problematic. Establish a screen-free routine at least one hour before bedtime. Instead, try reading a physical book, listening to calming music, doing light stretches, or practicing relaxation techniques. This helps signal to your brain that it's time to wind down and prepare for sleep.

A lower heart rate before bed indicates that your body is in a relaxed, parasympathetic state—ideal for quality sleep. Avoid intense exercise, stressful activities, or stimulating content in the evening. Practice calming activities like deep breathing exercises, progressive muscle relaxation, or gentle yoga to help lower your heart rate.

The Pomodoro Technique is a time management method that can be particularly effective for ADHD. Work in focused 25-minute intervals (called "Pomodoros") followed by 5-minute breaks. After completing four Pomodoros, take a longer 15-30 minute break. This technique helps maintain focus by breaking work into manageable chunks and provides regular rewards (breaks) to keep motivation high. The structured approach reduces overwhelm and makes it easier to start tasks, while the timer creates a sense of urgency that can help combat procrastination.

Light therapy using a 10,000 lux light box can help regulate circadian rhythms and improve sleep-wake cycles, which are often disrupted in people with ADHD. Exposure to bright light in the morning can increase alertness, improve mood, and help with focus throughout the day. Use the light box for 20-30 minutes each morning, ideally within the first hour of waking. Position it at eye level about 16-24 inches away. This can be especially helpful during darker winter months or if you struggle with morning alertness and delayed sleep phase syndrome.

PushMe is an Android app designed to help manage tasks and reminders through persistent notifications. For people with ADHD, out of sight truly means out of mind. This app keeps important tasks visible on your notification bar until you complete them, acting as a constant but gentle reminder. You can set up custom notifications for medication reminders, task deadlines, habit tracking, or any other important activities. The visual persistence helps combat the common ADHD challenge of forgetting tasks the moment they're not in immediate view.
